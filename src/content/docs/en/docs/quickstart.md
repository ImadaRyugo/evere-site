The basic flow in Evere is simply "**create a project → log expenses → settle up**." Here we'll walk through it using a trip as an example. It takes about 5 minutes.

## Step 1: Get started with Evere

Download Evere from the App Store and open it, then choose how to start.

- **Sign up with Apple / Google / email** — your data is saved to the cloud and all features (invites, recording settlements) are available.
- **Start as a guest** — use it right away without signing up, though some features are limited (see [Account & data](/en/docs/account/)).

First, set your **display name**. This is the name shown to your project members (you can change it later).

## Step 2: Create a project

Create a new project from the "Projects" tab.

1. Enter a **project name** (e.g., "Thailand Trip").
2. Choose the **currencies** you'll use. For an overseas trip, it's handy to include both the local currency (e.g., THB) and your own (e.g., USD).
3. One of the currencies you pick becomes the **base currency**. Settlement is calculated in this currency.
4. If you like, add **participants** by name (you can also add them later).

:::note
The base currency can no longer be changed once you log the first expense. Because it's used for all settlement calculations, double-check it when you create the project.
:::

## Step 3: Log an expense

Log expenses from the "Add" tab.

1. Enter the **amount** and **currency**.
2. Choose the **payer** (who paid).
3. Choose the **participants** (who shares this expense) and the **split mode** (equal, ratio, or manual).
4. Optionally add a category, title, date, memo, and receipt image.

Use "Add another" to record several expenses in a row.

## Step 4: Check the settlement status

Open the "Settlement" tab to see the result Evere calculated automatically.

- **Summary**: total number of expenses and total amount
- **Settlement status**: the ratio of settled vs. unsettled
- **Payment comparison**: for each participant, how much they paid vs. how much they owe
- **Settlement**: who should pay whom, and how much

## Step 5: Settle up and record it

Once the money has actually changed hands, tap that item on the settlement screen to mark it **settled**. Settled amounts are excluded from further calculations, and a record is kept of who has settled.

That completes the basic loop. From the next pages on, we'll look at each feature in more detail.
